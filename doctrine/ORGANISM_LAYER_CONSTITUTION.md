**Systems** are structured assemblies of Organs that form coherent domain platforms. Systems represent discrete capability units with defined boundaries.

**Organism** is the constitutionally self-regulating entity composed of Systems. Organism represents the entire platform as a unified, evolving, multi-system whole with cross-system governance and evolution mechanics.

---

### Platform vs Organism

**Platform** is a technical infrastructure concept referring to deployment environments, hosting capabilities, or ecosystem enablement mechanisms.

**Organism** is an architectural concept representing the constitutionally governed, self-regulating entity that transcends any single platform deployment. Organism defines the rules by which Systems evolve and interoperate.

---

### Ecosystem vs Organism

**Ecosystem** represents networks of interoperating Systems, external services, and participants. Ecosystems are emergent coordination patterns.

**Organism** is the constitutionally defined entity that governs the Systems within it. Organism provides the structural foundation upon which ecosystems may emerge.

---

### Product vs Organism

**Product** is a market-facing offering with branding, packaging, pricing, and go-to-market strategies. Products are commercial constructs.

**Organism** is the architectural entity that may power multiple Products. Organism is not a commercial construct but a constitutional platform entity.
